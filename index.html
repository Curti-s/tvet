About Tvet

Kenya’s Technical and Vocational Education and Training (TVET) system has a significant role to play to facilitate effective implementation of numerous planned projects, within key development sectors of manufacturing, infrastructure, science, technology and innovation, as highlighted within the Kenya Vision 2030, Medium Term Plan II (MTP II) (2013 – 2017). The Medium Term Plan II will be realized through enhancing both quality and relevance of the TVET system of education, training and research. This will in turn ensure that output from TVET system, whether skills, innovation capability or research capability, will be beneficial to the realization of goals highlighted within the identified sectors.

To achieve a “globally competitive quality education, training and research for sustainable development” within the TVET system, Kenya Vision 2030 places great emphasis on the link between education, training and the labour market, the need to create entrepreneurial skills and competencies and strong public and private partnerships.

Over the years, the TVETsystem has made significant progress in terms of development with more institutions being registered and the upgrade of both the Kenya Polytechnic and Mombasa Polytechnic to university colleges and ultimately to universities in January 2013. The TVET system initiated construction of 13 new public institutions as well as establishment of centres of excellence in order to provide quality and relevant skilled human resources in the middle level cadre
